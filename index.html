Science & Innovation

Katherine Johnson

Katherine Johnson was a NASA mathematician whose calculations were critical to the success of the first U.S. crewed spaceflights. She was born in 1918 in West Virginia and showed strong mathematical abilities from a young age. She graduated summa cum laude from West Virginia State College at age 18. She was married and had three daughters. Her story was featured in Hidden Figures.

Source: Wikipedia - Katherine Johnson, Image: NASA (public domain)

Jane C. Wright

Jane C. Wright was a pioneering cancer researcher and physician. She helped develop chemotherapy as a treatment for cancer and was instrumental in discovering how to test drugs on cancer cells. She earned her medical degree from New York Medical College. She was married and had two daughters.

Source: Wikipedia - Jane C. Wright, Image: public domain

Government & Politics

Kamala Harris

Kamala Harris is the first woman, the first African American, and the first Asian American Vice President of the United States. She attended Howard University and earned her law degree from UC Hastings. She is married to attorney Doug Emhoff.

Source: Wikipedia - Kamala Harris, Image: public domain

Shirley Chisholm

Shirley Chisholm was the first African American woman elected to Congress and the first woman to seek the Democratic nomination for U.S. President. She earned a master's degree in education from Columbia University. She was known for her fearless advocacy for minorities and women.

Source: Wikipedia - Shirley Chisholm, Image: public domain

Arts, Literature & Media

Toni Morrison

Toni Morrison was a Nobel Prize-winning author known for her vivid portrayal of African American life and identity. She earned degrees from Howard University and Cornell University. She authored novels such as Beloved and The Bluest Eye.

Source: Wikipedia - Toni Morrison, Image: public domain

Oprah Winfrey

Oprah Winfrey is a media mogul, philanthropist, and the first Black woman billionaire in the U.S. She studied at Tennessee State University. Her talk show became one of the most influential in history.

Source: Wikipedia - Oprah Winfrey, Image: public domain

Sports

Wilma Rudolph

Wilma Rudolph was the first American woman to win three gold medals in a single Olympic Games (1960). She overcame polio as a child and attended Tennessee State University.

Source: Wikipedia - Wilma Rudolph, Image: public domain

Serena Williams

Serena Williams is considered one of the greatest tennis players of all time. She has won 23 Grand Slam singles titles and is also a fashion designer and philanthropist.

Source: Wikipedia - Serena Williams, Image: public domain

Business & Innovation

Madam C.J. Walker

Madam C.J. Walker was the first female self-made millionaire in America. She developed a successful line of beauty and hair products for African American women.

Source: Wikipedia - Madam C. J. Walker, Image: public domain

Ursula Burns

Ursula Burns was the first Black woman to become CEO of a Fortune 500 company (Xerox). She earned degrees in mechanical engineering and became a major advocate for education and inclusion in business.

Source: Wikipedia - Ursula Burns, Image: public domain
